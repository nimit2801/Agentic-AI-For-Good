01 — Identity

What We Are

The Problem
Every builder with an AI idea spends hours every day prompting ChatGPT/Claude to research what tools already exist, what's open-source, what competitors are doing. The results are often hallucinated, incomplete, or outdated. There is no single, curated, trustworthy source for "what's already out there in agentic AI."

What We Do (Ordered by Priority)

  1. AI Tool Discovery & Knowledge Base — A curated, searchable directory of AI tools, open-source projects, and agentic systems. Categorized by use case, industry, and capability. This is the primary product.
  2. Offline Events — IRL meetups, college workshops, and community events. Key differentiator in India—nobody else is doing this.
  3. GTM Support for AI Tools — Help tools that need go-to-market support get discovered. Featured listings, sponsored stories, event presence. This is how we make money.
  4. Editorial Stories — Deep-dive case studies on AI tools in healthcare, education, climate, logistics. Adds depth and soul. NOT the highlight. Secondary focus.

04 — Investor Positioning

How to Position for Funding

Honest Truth
A content site about AI is NOT venture-backable as a media company. VCs learned their lesson with BuzzFeed, Vice, Mic. However, there are three investable framings—and grants should come first.

Framing 1: "The Bloomberg Terminal for AI Impact"

Thesis: As AI regulation increases globally, every company needs to demonstrate impact and responsible use. No structured, trusted source of truth exists. You build it.

CB Insights — $500M+ PitchBook — $225M acq. Crunchbase — $500M+ G2 — $1.1B

Framing 2: "AI-for-Good Talent Marketplace"

Thesis: Massive talent mismatch. AI engineers want meaningful work. Nonprofits can't find or afford AI talent. You become the connective tissue.

Framing 3: "Impact Verification Layer for AI"

Thesis: AI washing is the new greenwashing. You become the B Corp / LEED certification for AI impact.

Investor Targeting (Priority Order)

Tier 1: Grants (Start Here — No Equity)

  • Google.org AI for Social Good ($25M+ pool)
  • AWS AI for Changemakers Accelerator
  • Microsoft AI for Good grants
  • Mozilla Foundation / Responsible AI grants
  • Knight Foundation, Omidyar Network, MacArthur, Skoll

Target: $50K–$250K. They WANT to fund exactly what you're building.

Tier 2: Impact VCs (With Traction)

Obvious Ventures, Kapor Capital, Emerson Collective, DBL Partners

The Pitch Narrative

Problem

$258.7B invested in AI in 2025. No reliable way to find, verify, or compare tools that create real-world outcomes. Builders waste hours daily prompting AI for market research.

Solution

The definitive discovery platform for AI tools—starting with curated editorial, scaling to a structured database, ultimately becoming the trust layer for the AI ecosystem.

Why Now

EU AI Act enforcement begins 2026. AI washing backlash growing. Builders need a trustworthy, curated source. Nobody owns this space.

Building the Moat

  1. Data network effects: Every tool listed makes the directory more valuable
  2. Brand trust: First credible, design-forward voice in the space
  3. Community lock-in: Builders gather here, switching costs increase
  4. Offline events: Physical presence is hard to replicate at scale
  5. Relationships: Every featured company becomes a long-term partner

07 — Decisions

Decision Log

Major decisions and their reasoning. So future-you understands WHY.

Pivot to tool discovery platform Mar 17

Context: Original positioning was "stories of AI making real-world impact." Nice brand, hard to monetize.

Decision: Primary product = curated AI tool directory + knowledge base. Editorial stories = secondary (adds soul).

Reasoning: Solves a real daily pain point. Clearer monetization (tools pay for GTM). Comparable platforms prove the model.

Target India first, budget in INR Mar 17

Context: Nimit is based in India. Offline events are the growth differentiator.

Decision: Focus on Indian professionals and students first. Budget ~₹5.5L for 6 months.

Reasoning: Nobody else doing IRL AI tool events in India. College ambassadors are cheap + high reach.

Revenue = GTM services, not distribution Mar 17

Decision: Offer free listings + paid featured listings + sponsored stories + event sponsorships.

Reasoning: Can't sell audience you don't have yet. Sell content quality + association. Distribution becomes valuable later.

Grants before VCs Mar 17

Decision: Apply for grants first (Google.org, AWS, Mozilla). No equity dilution.

Reasoning: "AI for good" is exactly what grant programs fund. VCs later, only when data product has traction.

HashRouter fix is #1 priority Mar 17

Decision: Migrate to BrowserRouter before investing in any content/SEO.

Reasoning: All content investment is wasted if Google can't crawl the pages. Non-negotiable.
